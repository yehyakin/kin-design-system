# Product registers

Select one primary register for the surface being changed. Mixed products may use different registers by route, but one screen should not speak in several visual voices.

## Information site

Prioritize reading order, provenance, search, navigation, and content density. Use editorial hierarchy without turning the page into a marketing hero or a wall of equal cards.

Read `patterns/information-site.md` from the KIN repository before defining the page model.

## Product workspace

Prioritize entity identity, current state, recent activity, persistent navigation, keyboard efficiency, and inspector continuity. Design serves the task and should recede behind the data.

For monitoring, risk, evidence, or investigation products, read `patterns/intelligence-workspace.md`.

## Ecommerce operations

Prioritize product, inventory, campaign, order, channel, and approval state. Make money, quantity, schedule, source, and automation responsibility explicit. Do not confuse a creative preview with an operational record.

Read `patterns/ecommerce-operations.md` before defining commerce states or batch actions.

## Canvas or engineering tool

Prioritize the canvas, selection, mode, coordinates, revision, precision, and reversible editing. Chrome remains compact; direct manipulation needs continuous feedback and clear escape paths.

Read `patterns/engineering-canvas.md` before defining tools, selection, units, or generated changes.

## AI-assisted surface

AI does not define a separate visual genre. Apply the relevant product register, then add conclusion, evidence, uncertainty, action, approval, and rollback where AI participates.
